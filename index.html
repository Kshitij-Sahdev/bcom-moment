Key Findings

Species Differentiation:

  • The scatter plots reveal that iris species can be clearly differentiated based on their petal dimensions
  • Setosa species is completely separated from the other two species
  • Versicolor and Virginica show some overlap but are generally distinct

Measurement Correlations:

  • Strong positive correlation (0.96) between petal length and petal width
  • Moderate negative correlation (-0.43) between sepal width and petal length

Variable Importance:

  • Petal measurements are more useful than sepal measurements for species identification
  • Sepal width shows the most variation within species

Distribution Patterns:

  • Histograms show that setosa has the smallest petals
  • Virginica has the largest petals
  • Measurements follow approximately normal distributions within each species
