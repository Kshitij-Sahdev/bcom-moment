2. Dataset Variables

The iris dataset includes the following 5 variables:

Table 6: Variables in the Iris Dataset
Variable Type Description Range Units
Sepal.Length Numeric (continuous) Length of the sepal 4.3 - 7.9 cm
Sepal.Width Numeric (continuous) Width of the sepal 2.0 - 4.4 cm
Petal.Length Numeric (continuous) Length of the petal 1.0 - 6.9 cm
Petal.Width Numeric (continuous) Width of the petal 0.1 - 2.5 cm
Species Categorical (nominal) Species of iris flower 3 categories N/A

Variable Descriptions

  • Sepal Length: The length of the sepal (the green leaf-like structures that protect the flower in bud stage)
  • Sepal Width: The width of the sepal at its widest point
  • Petal Length: The length of the petal (the colorful parts of the flower)
  • Petal Width: The width of the petal at its widest point
  • Species: The taxonomic species of iris flower (setosa, versicolor, or virginica)

Key Findings

Species Differentiation:

  • The scatter plots reveal that iris species can be clearly differentiated based on their petal dimensions
  • Setosa species is completely separated from the other two species
  • Versicolor and Virginica show some overlap but are generally distinct

Measurement Correlations:

  • Strong positive correlation (0.963) between petal length and petal width
  • Moderate negative correlation (-0.428) between sepal width and petal length
  • Strong positive correlation (0.872) between sepal length and petal length

Variable Importance:

  • Petal measurements are more useful than sepal measurements for species identification
  • Sepal width shows the most variation within species
  • Virginica has the largest average measurements across most dimensions

Distribution Patterns:

  • Histograms show that setosa has the smallest petals
  • Virginica has the largest petals
  • Measurements follow approximately normal distributions within each species
